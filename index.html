What is Marinduque?

Marinduque is an island province in the Philippines located in Southwestern Tagalog Region or Mimaropa, formerly designated as Region IV-B. Its capital municipal of Boac. Marinduque lies between Tayabas Bay to the north and Sibuyan Sea to the South. It is west of the Bondoc Peninsula of Quezon province; East of Mindoro island; north of the island province of Romblon.
It is ranked number 1 by the Philippine National Police and Philippines Security Forces as the 2013 Most Peaceful Province in the country due to its low crime rate, also the Province is home to one of the oldest religious festivals of the country, the Moriones celebrated annually every Holy Week.
